About Iryna

I am a Marriage and Family therapist registered intern practicing under supervision of Don Hadlock, licensed MFT in Campbell, CA.

I have graduated from Institute of Transpersonal Psychology in 2014 with a Master's in Counseling Psychology and have been working with children and their families since then. I use a direct, experientialprocess-oriented approach in my work and combine it with Jungian, Humanistic, and Cognitive-Behavioral techniques.

I have experience of working with depression, psychosis, anxiety, trauma, and spirituality-related issues.

My Approach

I believe that each one of us has already experienced wholeness within at some point of our lives. It could have been during our childhood days, or during moments of absorption in our favorite hobby, or while enjoying a sunset, or having a spiritual experience. Most of us experienced moments of joy, connection, and feeling “enough”, at least for a few moments.

However, our environment is often non-supportive of this state. For most of us, our caretakers and early teachers were disconnected from their sense of wholeness and so modeled dysfunction and complacency. We grew up copying this way of being

the way of disconnection and boredom. Since wholeness is a state of being who you really are, when you are disconnected from this place, you forget your true nature. This forgetfulness leads you to confusion about what to do with your life and your career, jobs and relationships that don’t nurture you.

My goal is to help you reconnect to the sense of wholeness within you and help you remove the obstacles to that connection. I provide a supportive empathetic environment to explore what is blocking you to become your best self and fulfill your potential. I use an experiential and direct process-oriented approach focused on the present moment.
